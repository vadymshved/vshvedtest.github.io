About

A personal but polished introduction that presents science, teaching, and international development as one connected professional story.

Professional summary

I am an interdisciplinary chemist, chemistry educator, and teacher in training with an academic background spanning Ukraine, France, and Switzerland. My educational path includes a Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry, a Master’s degree in Applied Physics and Nanomaterials, and a second Master’s degree in Nanoscale Engineering from École Centrale de Lyon. I am currently enrolled at PHBern in the teaching diploma programme for upper secondary schools in chemistry, further strengthening my profile at the intersection of science, pedagogy, and international education.

Values and orientation

My work is shaped by clarity, curiosity, precision, openness, and long-term growth. I care about scientific depth, but I also care about how people understand, feel, and develop while learning. This gives my work a distinctly multilingual and intercultural dimension.

Clarity Curiosity Precision Openness Long-term growth

Longer biography

From an early age, I was drawn to science and especially to chemistry. During my school years, that interest grew through Olympiads, advanced study, and a lasting curiosity about how the natural world works. My school path was already deeply connected with science: I studied in a chemistry- and biology-focused environment, took part in regional and national chemistry competitions, and gradually discovered that chemistry was not only a subject I enjoyed, but a field that truly shaped the way I think.

This early passion led me to study Chemistry at Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv, where I built a strong scientific foundation and developed a growing interest in interdisciplinary science. Later, I continued with a Master’s degree in Applied Physics and Nanomaterials, and then completed a second Master’s degree in Nanoscale Engineering in France at École Centrale de Lyon. These studies gave me an academic path that connects chemistry with physics, nanotechnology, biology, materials science, and modern laboratory research. Today, I continue this journey in Switzerland, where I am training as a chemistry teacher for upper secondary schools at PHBern.

Alongside my studies, I have been teaching chemistry since the age of eighteen. What started with helping classmates and younger students gradually became a long-term commitment to tutoring, mentoring, and teaching learners from different countries and educational systems. Over the years, I have worked with school students, high school students, and university beginners, including preparation for demanding programs such as IB Diploma Chemistry. My teaching approach is shaped by both science and empathy: I believe chemistry should be taught clearly, patiently, and with real understanding, not just memorization.
